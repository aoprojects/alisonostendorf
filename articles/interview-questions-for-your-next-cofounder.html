Interview Questions To Ask a Potential Co-Founder

I am generally very pro-co-founder. Cofounders can fill in resource gaps, they can be fun to split up work with, and they can bring skills that compliment yours.

Investors prefer startups with multiple founders, and it's in general more fun to have a colleage "all in" with you.

But bringing on the wrong cofounder can cost more in the long run than you gain now at the founding phase. How can you know?


Please, honor the process.
Ask any potential cofounders the following questions. Have deep discussions about them. The company and your life will be much better off after workign through some of these hard topics.

  1. What is your money mentality? When to borrow money? When to take investments? What should we take from the company in income, and when?
  2. When to tell investors and clients about our product and how honest to be about our struggles during the process?
  3. Self care - how to manage "feeling good" and "feeling healthy enough" before we are net positive?
  4. What we are worth and how to demand it or stand up for it.
  5. What expenses should get paid by the company? When?
  6. What are your personal financial and domestic situations, and how it should those things the money we “need” in salary/income at early phases?
  7. When might we want to sell the company? Why?
  8. How to presell product?
  9. Data storage - can we put it in the cloud? How much do we care about data and security, and how do we take action on that?
  10. Marketing and messaging - who decides how, what, when, why, where to post? Do we all need to have a say in the process before it goes live?
  11. What is our visual branding, and how does it get "approved" by one another? Does it?
  12. Roles of our roles and how we override each other in times of conflict.
  13. How okay is it to fail, in front of customers and prospects? What would failure look like and how might we get around it?
  14. When will we get an office space?
  15. How to outsource? What to outsource? Who will manage that?
  16. SHould our equity be vested? If so, how do we measure that and who is in charge of measuring that?
  17. What documents do we need to create? How, by when, and where to store them? What will be not proceed with before writing and finalizing these documents?
  18. What is our scalability plan? Is it realistic for our market? Have we calculated it with numbers, or just qualitatiely?
  19. What are our Key Performance Indicators, and how often will we measure them? Where will we keep this data?
  20. When do we pay for outside expertise? When do we do things ourselves and potentially struggle through in order to save money? When do we hire a consultant? An employee?
  21. When do we fire said employees
  22. At what point might we stop trying, and either pivot or close the company?

Thanks for reading. I hope these questions prove themselves useful to you and your goals.
